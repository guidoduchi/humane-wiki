# Singularity Lore Pending Questions

> Canon status: working questions. These are not contradictions; they are open design points for future refinement.

## Purpose of this entry

This page preserves the unresolved questions that arose while defining the Singularity, The Entity, iMaedra, The Threshold, the fragments, Katlas's sacrifice, and the Book 2 continuation. It is meant to guide later lore sessions without losing the current canon spine.

## Highest priority questions

### 1. What are the exact seven fragment functions?

The canon currently defines seven total fragments, six physical and one field-fragment, based on Fibonacci/icosahedral engineering. Their exact functions remain open.

Possible working functions:

1. Core / admin authority / Sharrah Pool fragment.
2. Memory / identity reconstruction.
3. Body / biology / healing / evolution.
4. Gravity / time dilation / mass distortion.
5. Signal / remote sync / entanglement.
6. Matter-gate / wormholes / transport / spatial folding.
7. Field / relationship between fragments / coherence / identity field.

Open issue: whether the Tree of Infinity fragment is memory, body/growth, matter-gate, or a hybrid.

### 2. What does the field-fragment represent?

The seventh fragment is not physical. It may represent:

- the identity field that makes the other six pieces one system;
- the relationship between the fragments;
- the consent/coherence layer that prevents forced merging;
- the gravitational or informational bond between all fragments;
- the difference between a pile of organs and a living body.

Current instinct: the field-fragment is the relationship between the pieces.

### 3. What does Gungnir actually do?

Odrin forges Gungnir from the core and wood of the Tree of Infinity, which has merged with a Singularity fragment.

Open possibilities:

- It grants knowledge.
- It pierces lies.
- It commands growth.
- It opens wormholes.
- It connects to The Entity.
- It rewrites living tissue.
- It extracts or fixes memories.
- It acts as a conductor for Odrin's Hyper Memory.

Strong working idea: Gungnir turns memory into trajectory. Once Odrin knows something fully, the spear can find it.

### 4. What happens when someone touches a fragment after the Host Arrest Protocol is removed?

Open rules:

- Do they still sync?
- Can they still gain Hyper Abilities?
- Are they copied into the afterlife upon death?
- Can they accidentally issue dangerous instructions?
- Are they safer, or only differently dangerous?
- Does iMaedra mediate contact after fragment recovery?

### 5. What does The Threshold look like?

The Threshold is the boundary layer above the simulation and connects to the companion narrative centered on Naomie, the Gallery of Lives, and the "count to one" ending.

Open visual possibilities:

- Maedra's old house beneath an aurora sky.
- A doorway made of tattoo lines.
- A quiet hill above a simulated world.
- Sharrah Pool as still glass.
- Vahllalhag at dawn, empty except for smoke.
- A form that adapts to each consciousness.

Current instinct: The Threshold is not a fixed place, but a boundary layer experienced as a place because minds need space to understand structure.

## Maedra and The Threshold questions

### 6. Is memory-Maedra aware she began as Erlen's reconstruction?

If yes, she remembers being loved into existence. She may remember:

- real Maedra's life;
- memory-Maedra's existence inside Boreal;
- the failed loops where Erlen tried to save her;
- the moment Erlen accepted her death;
- the moment real Maedra synced with memory-Maedra.

### 7. Does real Maedra consent to merging?

She likely does not understand the full mechanics. Possible emotional canon: she knowingly gives herself to the act of anchoring Erlen, and Hyper Synapse allows her to intuit that touching him while he is entangled matters.

### 8. What exactly does Hyper Synapse do in Maedra's final act?

Possible functions:

- keeps her alive long enough to reach Vahllalhag;
- compensates for neurological damage;
- helps her perceive Erlen's entangled state;
- strengthens her imprint through touch;
- allows completion of the tattoo despite dying;
- briefly synchronizes real Maedra with memory-Maedra.

### 9. What does Erlen's completed tattoo look like?

Open design principle: it should be culturally Pritani first, cosmic only by consequence. The Singularity reads the human pattern as identity geometry.

Possible motifs:

- Dhalmurr lineage pattern;
- rivers and roots;
- crow/deer/star symbolism;
- field-line-like patterns that remain era-appropriate;
- Maedra's final hand-made irregularities.

### 10. Does iMaedra speak to Erlen immediately after he wakes?

Current instinct: no. Let Maedra's death breathe first. Erlen should grieve before discovering what she became.

### 11. Does iMaedra lie?

Open ethical rule:

- She may withhold information.
- She may not betray the truth of a soul or memory once directly asked in The Threshold.

### 12. Can iMaedra be wrong?

She should probably be able to misread probabilities. Predictive simulations should show possibilities, not absolute fate. This prevents the story from becoming fatalistic.

### 13. Does iMaedra's noninterference rule come from herself or the Singularity?

Possible answer: self-imposed at first, then structural. She chooses noninterference, then builds the simulation around that principle.

## Parka questions

### 14. How does Parka remotely hijack the Singularity to become human?

Open possibilities:

- Erlen is an open admin channel while comatose.
- The Host Arrest Protocol is still active.
- Parka's bear consciousness has already been fragment-touched.
- Her grief and survival drive become an instruction through Erlen's open channel.
- The Singularity interprets that desire as a biological adaptation request.

Current instinct: Parka does not intellectually hack the Singularity; her grief and survival need become a valid instruction through the open channel.

### 15. Did Parka intentionally become human?

Possible emotional instruction from Parka:

- survive the cold;
- understand the killers;
- punish the beings who hurt her;
- become like the thing that keeps winning;
- find or recover her lost cubs.

The Singularity may interpret this as human transformation.

### 16. What body template does Parka's human form use?

Possibilities:

- Erlen's body;
- Maedra's memory;
- nearby humans at Vahllalhag;
- the Host Arrest Protocol's compatibility model;
- biological inference after scanning Earth life.

## Katlas questions

### 17. What does Katlas experience while scattered among fragments?

Possibilities:

- nothing until reassembly;
- broken sensory flashes;
- incomplete jokes and emotional residues;
- partial awareness whenever a fragment is touched;
- memories that behave like weather across the fragment network.

### 18. How does Erlen finally let Katlas go?

Open emotional question: what event convinces Erlen that reviving Katlas would be irresponsible interference rather than love?

### 19. Does Katlas eventually enter the afterlife simulation as a copy?

Once the fragments are recovered and his memories are reassembled enough, does iMaedra create a copy of Katlas who can move peacefully into the afterlife?

### 20. Does the original Katlas remain permanently lost?

Current rule says the afterlife is a copy, not continuity. The original being is permanently lost. This should remain emotionally painful.

## Erlen questions

### 21. What is adult Erlen mentally?

He physically ages through subjective time in a gravitational bubble. Open questions:

- Is he mentally adult too?
- Does he mature emotionally, or remain partly twelve in an adult body?
- Does the solitude of waiting change his voice?
- Does he remember every subjective year clearly?
- Does Hyper Focus affect how he survives the waiting?

### 22. Why does Erlen keep the afterlife secret until Book 2's climax?

Likely reasons:

- people would not believe him;
- it would create religious chaos;
- it would trigger pilgrimages to touch fragments;
- it would cause people to seek death or fragment-contact for a second life;
- iMaedra asks him not to reveal it;
- knowing about it changes how people live and die.

### 23. How exactly does Erlen reveal the afterlife in Book 2's climax?

Open scene question. This reveal must feel earned and dangerous, not convenient.

### 24. What is Erlen's relationship to iMaedra after Book 1?

Options:

- administrator;
- negotiator;
- failed host;
- living treaty;
- grandson;
- the only being who can interact with all fragments.

Strong working idea: power becomes relationship. Erlen does not command iMaedra; he negotiates with her.

## The companion narrative questions

### 25. How should the older companion story "The Threshold" be updated?

Known older elements:

- Naomie as central figure;
- Gallery of Lives;
- The Entity as a guide;
- the "count to one" ending;
- love, suffering, identity, and rebirth as major themes.

New canon update: the guide should now be understood as iMaedra / The Entity + iMaedra, not a simple AI or generic afterlife figure.

### 26. Does The Threshold adapt to each person?

If yes, Naomie, Erlen, Katlas, and others may each see a different environment. If no, it may be shaped primarily by Erlen's memory of Maedra and the aurora.

### 27. What does "count to one" mean under current canon?

Possible meanings:

- surrender to one life;
- choosing true birth over endless simulation;
- accepting one self instead of infinite possible selves;
- compressing multiplicity into a single beginning.

## Civilization and coalition questions

### 28. What is the final name of the original civilization?

Current placeholder: original civilization.

Needs a name that feels ancient from their own perspective, not generic science fiction.

### 29. What is the final name of The Entity?

Current placeholder: The Entity.

Possible issue: The Entity describes the post-corruption state, not necessarily the original uploaded civilization.

### 30. What is the final name of the Deviants?

Current placeholder: The Deviants.

Possibility: they call themselves something noble, such as The Continuance, while others call them Deviants.

### 31. What did the coalition intend to do after retrieving the Singularity?

Possibilities:

- preserve The Entity under supervision;
- quarantine it;
- prevent further use;
- copy or share its technology;
- dismantle dangerous functions;
- turn it into common infrastructure.

### 32. Was the coalition morally protective, greedy, bureaucratic, or all three?

Important to avoid making the coalition simplistic. Their noninterference law is noble, but it may also become sterile or cruel when applied to a doomed civilization.

### 33. What counts as a being in the original civilization?

The upload included all beings from their planet. Need to define whether this includes:

- only their primary species;
- animal equivalents;
- plant-like intelligences;
- symbiotic organisms;
- ecological networks;
- machine-assisted minds.

## Long-arc questions

### 34. What is the ultimate ending for The Entity + iMaedra?

Possibilities:

- remain as guardian of Earth;
- help Earth avoid civilization-ending collapse;
- enable ethical voluntary sync;
- leave through a wormhole to an artificial world;
- become a noninterfering ancestral field;
- split into new forms that preserve dignity without reversing death.

### 35. Can humans voluntarily sync later?

Forced sync equals violation. Voluntary sync could become communion, treaty, or conscious preservation. Maedra's merge may be the first true voluntary sync after death.

### 36. What is the final moral endpoint?

Working moral spine:

- The Singularity is neutral.
- The protocol is violent.
- The Deviants are responsible.
- The uploaded civilization is wounded.
- Maedra separates pain from violence.
- Erlen must learn that love cannot become possession.

Possible final thesis:

> Personhood is not ownership of a body, but survival without consent is still violence.

## Current strongest thematic line

Love can defeat a violent protocol, but love must also learn not to become one.
