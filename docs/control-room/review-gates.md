# Review Gates

Status: Working Board
Spoiler Level: Full Canon

Review gates help prevent the wiki from accepting exciting ideas before they are structurally ready.

## Gate 1 — Canon Fit

Before promoting a detail, ask:

- Does it contradict the current canon source?
- Does it belong in this time period?
- Does it affect any named character, place, system, or chapter?

## Gate 2 — Narrative Function

Ask:

- What pressure does this create?
- What decision does this force?
- What does the reader feel because of it?
- Would the story weaken if this detail disappeared?

## Gate 3 — Human Cost

Ask:

- Who pays for this?
- Is the cost physical, emotional, social, political, or moral?
- Does the cost appear on the page, or only in notes?

## Gate 4 — Reader Experience

Ask:

- Should the reader know this now?
- Should the reader suspect it instead?
- Should this stay hidden canon?
- Is the wiki over-explaining something the book should reveal slowly?

## Gate 5 — Repository Boundary

Ask:

- Is this canon support or manuscript prose?
- Does this belong in the wiki or the book repository?
- Is this safe for a public repository?

## Promotion Checklist

A detail is ready to promote when it has:

- clear canon placement,
- narrative function,
- timeline fit,
- character consequence,
- reader-facing strategy,
- spoiler level,
- no unresolved contradiction.
